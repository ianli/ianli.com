Hi, I’m Ian Li .

I like making things. I like turning ideas into reality. On this website, I share my projects and drawings, including my research and writings during my Ph.D. Making things is also my daytime job as a UX engineer at Google.

Projects

I've enjoyed making things since I was a kid. I turned everyday items into toys. A matchbox became a robot, scrapwood into a boomerang, a shampoo bottle into a hydroplane. As I've become older, the joy of making has remained with me, albeit with different materials.

Drawings

When I was young, I was obsessed with comic books (Spider-Man being my absolute favorite). I wanted to be able to draw from imagination without "copying". I thought comic book artists drew that way and so must I. Over time, my interest in comic books waned, but I continued to draw. Over the years, I even learned to paint from real life.

Ph.D.

I was a Ph.D. student at Carnegie Mellon University in the Human-Computer Interaction Institute from 2004 to 2011. During that time, I worked on designing and developing tools that help people track and visualize information about their behavior. I eventually called this research area, personal informatics. My work was advised by the wisdom and support of my advisors Anind Dey and Jodi Forlizzi.
